TRIP GENERATION

-- > TRIP GENERATION < --

Do you know that trip generation is a transportation planning concept that refers to the process of predicting the number of trips (i.e., the number of vehicles or people) that will be generated by a land use or development. This is an important step in the transportation planning process because it provides information about the amount and nature of transportation demand that a particular land use or development will generate. The accuracy of the trip generation predictions is important because it can affect the efficiency, safety, and sustainability of the transportation system.

TRIP DISTRIBUTION

--> TRIP DISTRIBUTION < --

Trip distribution is crucial in transportation planning, estimating trips between locations. Planners analyze demographic data, travel behavior, and land use to create models. These models unveil movement patterns within the study area. Understanding these patterns aids decision-making processes. By identifying trends, planners can inform policies and infrastructure development. Ultimately, this process guides efficient transportation networks.

-- > MODAL SPLIT < --

Do you know that the Modal split is that aspect of the demand analysis process that determines the number (or percentage) of trips between zones that are made by automobile and transit. The selection of mode is a complex process that depends on various factors such as income, availability of transit service, time, cost, comfort, and convenience.

-- > TRAFFIC ASSIGNMENT < --

Do you know that Traffic assignment models are used to estimate the traffic flows on a network? These models take as input a matrix of flows that indicate the volume of traffic between origin and destination (O-D) pairs. The fundamental aim of the traffic assignment process is to reproduce on the transportation system, the pattern of vehicular movements which would be observed when the travel demand represented by the trip matrix, or matrices, to be assigned is satisfied.
